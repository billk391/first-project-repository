Introduction

Hi my name is Bill Kavanagh and this is a website I have made about the history of my home which is located in the south west of county Wicklow near the Carlow and Wexford borders.

The coming of the Kavanaghs to the house

My ancestors moved into the house around the year 1898. At that time the house was owned by the Fitzwilliam estate. My great great grandmother moved into the house having been forced to leave her old home due to being a widow and having no rights. She moved in with her only son Daniel Kavanagh who was my great grand father.

The Fitzwilliams

The Fitzwilliams were one of the wealthiest families in Britain and owned about one fifth of County Wicklow at the time my great great grandmother came to the house. The of the Fitzwilliams died in a plane crash alongside his beloved Kick Kennedy who was the older sister of Bobby Kennedy and Jack Kennedy the former president of the United States.

The Farm

The house has alwasy been a farmhouse and while much of the land is used for grazing livestock at the moment it was always used to grow crops like:

  • potatoes
  • turnips
  • cereals
Back then people had to eat whatever they could grow so it was a very different existance back then. Running water only came to the house in the late in the early 1950's.

Former Tenants of the house the Plunkets

The former owners were a family of Plunkets in which there were three siblings, two men and one woman all unmarried.
